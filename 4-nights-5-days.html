EXPLORE

4 NIGHTS / 5 DAYS

Dambulla – Sigiriya – Kandy – Nuwara Eliya – Kitulgala – Colombo – Airport

Day 01 – Airport – Dambulla

On the first day of your visit to wonderful Sri Lanka, we suggest you visit the famous Dambulla Cave Temple – a UNESCO World Heritage Site in the heart of Sri Lanka.

Dambulla Cave Temple

Dambulla cave temple also known as the Golden Temple of Dambulla is a World Heritage Site (1991) in Sri Lanka, situated in the central part of the country. This site is situated 148 kilometres east of Colombo and 72 kilometres north of Kandy.

There are more than 80 documented caves in the surrounding area. Major attractions are spread over five caves, which contain 153 Buddha statues, three statues of Sri Lankan kings and four statues of gods and goddesses. The murals cover an area of 2,100 square metres.

Day 02 – Sigiriya – Matale – Kandy

On the second day, visit the magnificent Sigiriya Rock Fortress in the morning. Later, visit the Matale Spice Garden on the way to Kandy.

Sigiriya

Sigiriya or Sinhagiri is an ancient rock fortress located in the northern Matale District near the town of Dambulla in the Central Province, Sri Lanka. It is dominated by a massive column of rock approximately 180 m (590 ft) high.

Built by King Kashyapa (AD 477–495), the palace on top of this rock was decorated with colorful frescoes. A gateway was built in the form of an enormous lion halfway up the rock. Sigiriya today is a UNESCO-listed World Heritage Site.

Kandy

Kandy is a major city in Sri Lanka located in the Central Province. It was the last capital of the ancient kings' era of Sri Lanka. Kandy is the home of the Temple of the Tooth Relic (Sri Dalada Maligawa), one of the most sacred places of worship in the Buddhist world. It was declared a world heritage site by UNESCO in 1988.

Day 03 – Peradeniya – Pinnawala – Nuwara Eliya

After breakfast, we will take you to the Peradeniya Botanical Garden and Pinnawala Elephant Orphanage. Then proceed to the magical hill country of Nuwara Eliya.

Peradeniya Botanical Garden

Peradeniya Botanical Garden lies in a spiral of the Mahaweli River and is 6 km west of Kandy. The history of this spectacular botanical garden dates back to the 14th century. The garden was established in 1821 and spans over 147 acres with over 4,000 species of plants.

Pinnawala Elephant Orphanage

Pinnawala Elephant Orphanage is an orphanage, nursery, and captive breeding ground for wild Asian elephants located at Pinnawala village, 13 km northeast of Kegalle town. Pinnawala has the largest herd of captive elephants in the world, established in 1975 by the Sri Lanka Department of Wildlife Conservation.

Day 04 – Nuwara Eliya – Kitulgala

Enjoy the beautiful tea country scenery of Nuwara Eliya in the morning. Then head to Kitulgala – Sri Lanka's adventure capital famous for white water rafting and the filming location of "The Bridge on the River Kwai".

Nuwara Eliya

Nuwara Eliya meaning "city on the plain" or "city of light" is a town in the central highlands of Sri Lanka. It is one of the major tea-producing areas in the world. The tallest mountain in Sri Lanka "Pidurutalagala" oversees this beautiful city. It is the most visited hill country destination.

Kitulgala

Kitulgala is a small town in Kegalle District, located on the banks of the Kelani River in the wet zone of Sri Lanka. It is most famous as the location where the Academy Award-winning film "The Bridge on the River Kwai" was filmed in 1957.

Today, Kitulgala is a popular destination for white water rafting and adventure sports. The Kelani River offers exciting rapids suitable for beginners and experienced rafters alike. The surrounding rainforest is also ideal for bird watching, jungle trekking, and canyoning. It is home to many endemic bird species found only in Sri Lanka.

Day 05 – Kitulgala – Colombo – Airport

After breakfast, we drive to Colombo for some city sightseeing. Then you will be taken to the airport for your upcoming flight. Hoping you have enjoyed your wonderful Sri Lanka experience!
